TCP

Transmission Control Protocol (TCP) is one of the main protocols of the Internet Protocol Suite. It is designed to provide a reliable, ordered, and error- checked delivery of a stream of bytes between applications running on hosts communicating over an IP network. Fundamental to the design of TCP is the establishment of a bi-directional communication channel that ensures data integrity through acknowledgments and retransmissions. This protocol facilitates the internet's core functions such as browsing, email, and file transfer, ensuring that data sent and received is consistent and accurate.

To initiate a TCP connection you have to first have a starter procedure called the three-way handshake.
